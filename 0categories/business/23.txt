Cobham accepts £4bn takeover offer
obham, the British aerospace group, has accepted a £4 billion takeover bid from an American buyout specialist.

Advent International, the private equity firm, has swooped on the troubled FTSE 250 business with an offer that represents a 34 per cent premium to yesterday’s share price.

The shares jumped to a three-year high this morning after the board accepted the bid of 165p a share.

Cobham, founded in 1934 by the aviation pioneer Sir Alan Cobham, employs more than 11,500 people worldwide. It is the world leader in producing hoses and the conical drogues that enable tanker aircraft to refuel fighter jets mid-flight.

Its shares have tumbled over recent years as a string of profit warnings forced it into two rights issues. They surged this morning
